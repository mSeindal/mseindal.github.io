M.Sc. Signal Processing and Acoustics

Software engineer at Samsung Denmark Research Center

In June 2020 I finished my master in Signal Processing and Acoustics at Aalborg University and since I've worked as an acoustical engineer at SWECO Denmark and as software engineer at Samsung Denmark Research Center. Through my study, my main focus have been on acoustical signal processing, such as beamforming, filter design and testing system, but now I'm working with bluetooth and power testing in our Bluetooth low-level team. My motivations are developing, making tasks where you have to think out of the box and solving problems by creating new solutions, or just make some already existing solutions smarter/in a different way.

University Projects

Some of the funniest from M.Sc. and B.Sc.

Investigation of Personal Sound Zones by Use of Subarray Division
Made a method for dividing a speaker array into subarrys in suboptimal sound zone positions. Simulated in MATLAB and tested on a real world setup. A video of the project can be found on my YouTube Channel.
3D Sound Localization Performance in a Virtual Environment With Movement
An investigation in how people locates sound in a game when using different HRTF data sets. Made a large test setup with 40 participants.
Real Time Vocal Effects for Live Use
Implementation and designing real time vocal effect on a DSP. Made filter design for general audio effects such as: reverb, equalizer, compressor etc.
Platooning Car System
Designing and implementing a platooning car system on a RC car. Worked with control systems, transfer functions, CAN-bus protocol and real time systems.
